I grew up with a guitar in one hand and a controller in the other. At some point, I put them both down and picked up a job title instead.

Two jobs. A lumber business. A husband and a father. Someone who knows what it means to carry real weight and still hear that older voice asking: is this all there is?

The Sunday Strategy came out of the margins — early mornings, stolen Sundays, questions I kept returning to about leverage and what it costs to build a life that actually belongs to you.

I'm writing for the man who's already doing a lot, and suspects he could do it all differently if he just had a clearer map.
